SPIDER SENSE - ROUNDS

PRELIMINARY ROUND

The first round of the competition. Any number of participants can take part in the preliminary round of spider sense. The participants are provided with design components which they are supposed to replicate using CSS in the given time frame. Five questions are given to each team, members of the team are supposed to finish the design within the specified time limit. The first 10 teams to successfully complete the challenge advance to the intermediate round. Judging criteria would solely be based on the accuracy and the fastest delivery of the designs

Duration of the round: 30mins

INTERMEDIATE ROUND

In the second round of Spider Sense, the messed-up frontend of a full stack web app will be given to each of the teams. The members of the team are supposed to correct the design and should be able to revert the design to make it look like the original frontend of the project. Higher points will be awarded to the team’s whose design closely resembles the actual frontend. The first 5 teams to finish the challenge will advance to the final round

Duration of the round: 45mins

FINAL ROUND

In the final round of Spider Sense, the erroneous backend of the same full stack web app will be given to the final contestants. The teams are supposed to get the website up and running. The teams are supposed to add database operations to the web app within the given time frame. Accuracy and speed are the judging criteria of the round.

Duration of the round: 1hr 15mins

GENERAL RULES

  1. Participants won’t be allowed to use frameworks other than the ones provided by the organising team
  2. Participants should have basic knowledge about HTML, CSS, JS, NODE.
  3. Maximum of 10 teams will advance to the intermediate round and 5 teams will advance to the final round.
  4. Maximum of 4 members are allowed per team.
  5. Database and ORM can be used as per the preference of the participants.
  6. The products of final rounds will be collected through github.

JUDGING CRITERIA

ROUND 1

Participants are supposed to solve the questions directly on the CSSBattle website, ranking provided by the website will be the considered for evaluation. Top 10 teams with the highest average will advance to the final round. If the team consists of 3-4 members, 4 questions will be provided. If the team consist of 2 or less members, 3 questions will be provided. The Score of each design is considered and the average score obtained by each team will be the qualifying factor.

ROUND 2

A Figma file of the web design to be developed will be provided to the participants. The objective is to develop a frontend that is same as the given design. Participants are allowed a single submission after which no changes can be made. The top 5 teams finishing the design with accuracy and lowest development time will advance to the finals The frontend that bears the maximum resemblance with the provided design will be awarded higher points. Judging factors are Faster delivery, Responsiveness of the website, functionality of the components, working flow of the website.

ROUND 3

The final round is a race against time. Participants are provided with incomplete backend code along with the schema. Participants should figure out the code and connect the frontend they developed in the previous round to the given backend The finished product should be the same as the demonstrated project. Fully functional webapp with CRUD operations should be demonstrated. Time will be the tie breaking factor.
